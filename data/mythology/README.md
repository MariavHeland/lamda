# LAMDA Story Structure & Mythology Database

## Purpose

This database gives LAMDA two capabilities no other screenplay analysis tool has:

1. **Analyze screenplays through narrative traditions beyond Hollywood's three-act structure.** When a screenplay's structure echoes a non-Western tradition, LAMDA identifies it and evaluates it on its own terms.

2. **Diagnose structure at every level** — from the macro (which tradition/grammar is this?) to the micro (does this scene turn?) — informed by the world's wisest script doctors, the boldest art house filmmakers, and 5,000 years of mythological storytelling.

## HOW LAMDA SHOULD USE THIS

### The Research Engine:
- **Identify the tradition**: Is this screenplay operating in three-act Western structure, kishōtenketsu, cyclical mythology, or something else?
- **Find the right comps**: Don't just suggest Hollywood comps. If the screenplay echoes Yoruba griot tradition, suggest Yeelen, Atlantics. If it follows kishōtenketsu, suggest Ozu, Kore-eda.
- **Map the archetypes**: Go beyond Campbell. Is this a trickster protagonist? A communal protagonist? A reluctant hero?
- **Position on the spectrum**: Where does this sit between archplot, miniplot, and antiplot?

### The Script Doctor:
- **Diagnose at every level**: Macro (which grammar?), meso (do sequences build?), micro (does this scene turn?)
- **Respect the tradition**: A kishōtenketsu screenplay's "lack of conflict" is not a flaw. A Norse-structure screenplay's predetermined ending is not a lack of suspense.
- **Apply the five universals**: Is knowledge managed well? Do stakes escalate within the story's own logic? Is form fused with content? Does the ending recontextualize? Is the audience's experience considered?
- **Use the "surprising yet inevitable" checklist** for turns and endings
- **Reference specific films** that solved the same structural challenge

### The Key Insight:
**Structure is not a formula. Structure is the organization of meaning in time.** Every tradition in this database is a different answer to the same question: how do you arrange human experience so that it reveals truth? LAMDA's job is to recognize which answer a screenplay is giving, and help the writer give it better.
